# Write a prompt asking the same question (e.g., "What is climate change?").

The Prompt- What is climate change?

- Climate change refers to long-term shifts in Earth’s average temperatures and weather patterns, mainly caused today by human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes. These activities release greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide that trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming and changes in climate systems.

Its effects include rising temperatures, melting ice caps, stronger storms, sea-level rise, droughts, and changes to ecosystems and agriculture. While climate has naturally changed throughout history, current changes are happening much faster due to human influence.


# Add role instructions for at least 3 personas, such as:

### Scientist → technical explanation:

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns, primarily driven by anthropogenic greenhouse gas emissions since the Industrial Revolution. The mechanism involves radiative forcing increased concentrations of CO₂, CH₄, and N₂O trap infrared radiation in the troposphere, disrupting the Earth's energy balance.

***Key Data Points:***
- Global temperatures have risen ~1.1°C above pre-industrial levels
- CO₂ concentrations exceed 420 ppm (highest in 800,000 years)
- Arctic ice is declining at 13% per decade
- Sea levels rising 3.3mm annually

***Core Mechanisms:***
- Greenhouse effect amplification
- Albedo reduction from ice loss
- Permafrost methane release (feedback loops)
- Ocean acidification (pH dropped from 8.2 to 8.1)

***Scientific Consensus:*** 97% of climate scientists agree human activity is the primary driver.




### Journalist → informative for the public:

#### Our Planet is Changing And Here's What You Need to Know
Climate change is one of the biggest stories of our generation. Put simply, our planet is getting warmer and human activity is the primary cause.

***The Simple Truth:*** Since the Industrial Revolution, factories, cars, and power plants have pumped carbon dioxide and other gases into our atmosphere. These gases act like a blanket around Earth, trapping heat that would otherwise escape into space.

***What's Happening Right Now:***
- Extreme weather events are becoming more frequent
- Wildfires are burning longer and hotter
- Coastal communities face rising sea levels
- Farmers struggle with unpredictable rainfall patterns

***Who's Affected?*** Everyone but especially vulnerable communities in developing nations who contribute least to the problem but face the harshest consequences.

***What Are Leaders Doing?*** World governments met at COP28 to discuss emissions targets, but critics argue action isn't fast enough.

***The Bottom Line:*** Scientists warn we have a narrow window to limit warming to 1.5°C. The decisions made today will shape the world your children inherit.




### Critic → skeptical tone:

#### Climate Change: Separating Facts from Hysteria
Let's be clear the climate is changing. It always has. What deserves scrutiny is the narrative being sold to the public.

***What We're Told vs. What's Complicated:***
- "97% scientific consensus" but consensus on WHAT exactly? That humans contribute? Yes. That apocalypse is imminent? That's a political statement, not purely scientific.
- "Unprecedented warming" Earth has experienced ice ages and warming periods long before humans existed
- "We have 12 years left" Deadlines keep shifting. Remember 2000? 2012? Now 2030?

***Questions Worth Asking:***
- Who funds climate research?
- Why do climate models consistently overpredict warming?
- Why are solutions always government intervention and taxation?
- Why are nuclear energy advocates silenced in "green" discussions?

***What's Being Ignored:***
- Developing nations need cheap energy NOW
- Climate policy disproportionately hurts the poor
- Technology, not panic, has historically solved environmental problems

***The Real Issue:*** Climate change is real. Climate catastrophism may be oversold. Healthy skepticism isn't denial, it's intellectual responsibility.

### Key Differences I Noticed:
The scientist response focused more on accuracy, evidence, and technical clarity, while the journalist response focused on accessibility and public understanding. The critic response on the other hand shifts focus toward questioning assumptions and framing the issue as a debate or arguement rather than a settled explanation. 
Each role changes not just tone but also which facts and perspectives are elaborated on.



### What I Learned About Role Prompting:
Role instructions strongly influence the tone, vocabulary, and the type of reasoning the AI uses. By defining a role, it guides the AI’s focuse on whether clarity, neutrality, persuasion, or critical analysis. Role prompting basically helps the AI tailor or deliver outputs to diverse audiences and goals.
