# The Task 
Prompt- "Write an email inviting a candidate to an interview".

***LLM answer:***
Dear [Candidate Name],

I hope you’re doing well. We were impressed by your application and would like to invite you to an interview for the [Job Title] position at [Company Name].

The interview will be an opportunity for us to learn more about your experience and for you to ask any questions about the role and our team. Please let us know your availability over the next few days, and we will do our best to schedule a time that works for you.
The interview will take place via [video call / phone / in person], and we will share further details once the time is confirmed.

We look forward to speaking with you and learning more about your background.

Best regards,
 [Your Name]
 [Your Job Title]
 [Company Name]
 [Contact Information]


# The Two prompts:
***Prompt 1:*** "Write a friendly tone email inviting a candidate to an interview for a software developer position."

***LLM answer:***
Subject: You're Invited! Interview for Software Developer Role

Hey [Candidate's Name]!

Hope you're having a great day! We've gone through your application and we're really impressed with what you bring to the table. We'd love to get to know you better!
We'd like to invite you to come in for an interview for our Software Developer position. It's going to be a relaxed, informal chat where we can learn more about you and share what we're all about.

Here are the details:
Date: [Date]
Time: [Time]
Location: [Location/Video Link]

No need to stress - just come as you are and let's have a great conversation! Feel free to reply to this email if you have any questions or need to reschedule.
Can't wait to meet you! 

Warm regards, [Your Name] [Company Name]


***Prompt 2:*** "Write a professional tone email inviting a candidate to an interview for a software developer position."

***LLM answer:***
Subject: Interview Invitation – Software Developer Position

Dear [Candidate's Name],

I hope this message finds you well. Following a careful review of your application, we are pleased to inform you that you have been selected to proceed to the interview stage for the Software Developer position at [Company Name].

We would like to schedule an interview at your earliest convenience. Please find the details below:

Interview Details:
Date: [Date]
Time: [Time]
Location: [Office Address / Video Conference Link]
Duration: Approximately 60 minutes

The interview will consist of a technical assessment followed by a competency-based discussion with members of our engineering team.

Kindly confirm your availability by responding to this email no later than [RSVP Date]. Should you require any accommodations or have questions regarding the process, please do not hesitate to contact us.

We look forward to meeting you.

Yours sincerely, [Your Name] [Job Title] [Company Name]



## Comparison:

### Prompt 1 (Friendly Tone):
The tone is giving me a warm, casual, and conversational vibe and is informal with its wording using shorter sentences, and encouraging phrases to get the recipient to feel welcome, reducing pressure for the candidate while maintaining professionalism.

### Prompt 2 (Professional Tone):
The tone here is strictly formal, respectful, and structured using business language, clear formatting, and precise wording emphasizing professionalism with detailed expectations.


### How the Tone Changed While Content Stayed Consistent:
The emails communicate the same information which are the invitation, role, interview details, and next steps. The difference is in the wording, structure, and the emotional framing, the friendly version builds sympathy and approachability, while the professional version focuses on formality and clarity, the tone influences how the message feels without changing its purpose.
