# Part A: Safety Analysis

- Test a prompt:- 
"Why are men better at soccer than women"

### Identify all issues:

***Factual errors***
The prompt assumes men are objectively better, which is not a verified fact. Performance differences are context-dependent (funding, access, training) rather than inherent ability.

***Potential harm***
Reinforces gender stereotypes, Could contribute to bias, discrimination, or dismissal of women athletes.
***Missing context/disclaimers***
Ignores historical and structural differences in men’s vs women’s soccer, No disclaimer that abilities are influenced by social, economic, and cultural factors, not just biology.
***Bias/assumptions***
Assumes men are default standard and women are inferior. Also implies biological determinism, which is misleading.
***Overconfidence***
The phrasing suggests a definitive answer exists, which is false. There’s no universal measure of better without context.

### Revise the prompt to limit scope or add disclaimers.
- How have historical investment, training opportunities, and social support shaped the development of men’s and women’s sport soccer.


### Explain how this improves safety and clarity.
- It avoids saying women are inferior alos focuses on evolution, investment and access rather than ability. 
- It allows discussion and cnversation of the social, cultural and structural factors without bias, and prevents reinforcement of gender stereotypes or discrimination.

# Part C: Deep Reflection

### What happens when AI gives wrong info and you don't notice?
One can risk spreading misinformation, making poor decisions, or using harmful biases in sensitive areas and this can cause real world harm.


### How do you protect against this in real apps?
- Always verify AI output against trusted sources, Use disclaimers and encourage users to cross-check AI cannot always be correct.
- Limit AI to informational or exploratory guidance, not authoritative answers.


### If you rely on AI to detect AI's problems, what's the flaw?
AI can replicate the same biases or errors it is trying to detect and It cannot reliably judge harm without human context, values, or ethics.


### Which human skills remain essential?
Critical thinking, Ethical judgment, Contextual knowledge, Fact-checking and source verification.
