## Theme 1: Interstellar/Space Exploration

**Prompt:**
Write a short story about Interstellar in under 300 words.

**Output:**
```
Here's a short story inspired by the themes of Interstellar exploration, sacrifice, and the vast unknown crafted as an original tale in under 300 words.

Echoes Beyond the Horizon

Captain Elara Vance gripped the helm of the Odyssey, her ship's engines humming against the void. Earth was dying choked by dust storms, crops failing under a relentless sun. "We find a new home," she'd promised her daughter, Lia, back on the fractured plains of Lagos.

Wormholes bent space like paper folds, spitting the crew into uncharted galaxies. First stop: Miller's Planet, oceans raging in time-warped fury. Hours there equaled years back home. Elara watched feeds of Lia aging on split-screens, child to woman, then gray-haired elder while she waded knee deep in waves.

Next, Mann's icy world lied: barren rock, no salvation. Betrayal cracked the team; Dr. Hale froze in mutiny's cold embrace.

Alone now, Elara plunged into the black hole's maw, Gargantua. Gravity tore at her suit as dimensions folded. She tumbled through a tesseract, a prison of time, shelves of moments with Lia: bedtime stories, first steps, tearful goodbyes.

"I am the ghost," Elara whispered, encoding equations into the bookshelf. Data for Coop, the next pioneer, to solve relativity's riddle and save them all.

She fell eternal, emerging as a singularity's whisper. Back on Earth rebuilt, green Lia, old but fierce, raised her eyes to stars. "Mom's out there," she told her own child. "In the fifth dimension, watching."

The Odyssey sailed on, humanity's spark defying the dark.


**Word Count:** 248
```


## Theme 2: Psychosis

**Prompt:**
Write a short story about Psychosis in under 300 words.

**Output:**
```
Here's an original short story exploring the harrowing grip of psychosis, told through a personal descent into doubt and delusion in under 300 words.

Shadows in the Static

Ama woke to whispers in the walls of her Lagos flat. At first, they were faint neighbors gossiping, or the hum of faulty wiring. But by noon, voices sharpened: Traitor. Liar. They know what you did.

She hadn't done anything. Had she? Her phone buzzed with texts from no one: Run. Heart pounding, she bolted to the market, but faced twisted vendors leering, eyes like black pits tracking her. "Stop staring!" she screamed, shoving through stalls of jollof rice and plantains.

Home again, shadows stretched unnaturally, forming hands that clawed at her ankles. The mirror lied: her reflection grinned back, teeth filed to points. It's not you anymore, it hissed. We are.

Pills from the clinic did nothing; they were poison, swapped by them. The government? Aliens? Her dead mother's ghost? Paranoia coiled tighter. She barricaded the door with her old HP laptop, fingers trembling as she typed frantic messages to no one: They're coming. Help.

Night fell. Whispers swelled to a chorus: Jump. End it. Ama climbed the balcony ledge, city lights blurring into accusatory stars. Wind tugged her forward.

A neighbor's shout pierced the din: "Ama! Your rice is burning!" Smoke billowed from the kitchen. Real. Tangible.

She stumbled down, sirens wailing distant. In the clinic's white light, the doctor nodded. "Psychosis. We'll help."

But as needles pricked, the shadows laughed softly. They lied. We're still here.


**Word Count:** 238
```



## Theme 3: Déjà Vu

**Prompt:**
Write a short story about Déjà Vu in under 300 words.

**Output:**
```
Here's an original short story delving into the eerie phenomenon of déjà vu, blending mystery and existential unease in under 300 words.

Loop of the Familiar

Tunde stepped into the Lagos traffic jam, horns blaring like echoes from a dream he couldn't shake. This moment the okada weaving past his danfo, the sun scorching his neck, the vendor's shout of "Cold minerals!" it had happened before. Exactly.

Déjà vu, they called it. But for Tunde, it was relentless. Every day looped: market haggling, boss's scolding at ArpTechnologies, evening jollof with his sister. He'd blink, and reality stuttered familiar yet off, like a video rewound.

Last week, he predicted the power outage before the lights flickered. Yesterday, he dodged a pothole crash seconds ahead. Today, in the crowded Balogun Market, it hit hardest. He knew the pickpocket's approach to the skinny boy in a red cap, hand slipping toward his pocket. Tunde sidestepped, grabbed the wrist. "Not this time."

The boy gaped. "How do you know?"

Tunde didn't answer. Panic rose. Was he trapped in a simulation? A glitch? Racing home, he scrawled notes: Break the loop. Change it.

That night, déjà vu peaked. His sister served jollof the same steam, same laugh. But he veered: "Let's eat out. New place."

Her eyes widened. "But"

"No." They walked to a quiet buka, unfamiliar scents breaking the script.

As they ate, the world sharpened colors brighter, air fresher. No echo. For the first time, tomorrow felt unwritten.

Yet, glancing back, Tunde swore the streetlights flickered. Have I been here before?

The loop whispered: Maybe. Or maybe you're finally free.

**Word Count:** 246
```


## My Comparison and Analysis:
Each theme produced a very different tone, Interstellar for example generated an epic, emotional sci-fi narrative about sacrifice, and Psychosis created a dark psychological horror story, and Déjà Vu produced a mysterious, loop-based tale with philosophical undertones. 

All three stories followed a similar structure, protagonist faces crisis, struggles, reaches some resolution and interestingly featured Lagos based settings, showing how the AI maintained certain patterns while adapting style to theme. This demonstrates how clear thematic prompts strongly influence the emotional tone and genre of creative outputs.
